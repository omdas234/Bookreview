The Personal MBA: Book Review

By Rajendra Das

Many people around the world now wish to pursue an MBA, but due to the cost, time, and effort involved, not everyone can do so. Josh Kaufman was one of them when he joined P&G as assistant brand manager and saw many colleagues holding an MBA from a prestigious source. He, himself never studying business, took a different approach.

How The Personal MBA Started

Inspired by Charlie Munger, Warren Buffet's business partner, Josh started reading books from top business schools. The more he read these books, the more he realized they revolved around common principles. He simply put all of these concepts in a book as a shortcut to an MBA called The Personal MBA.

The Main Idea of the Book: The 5 Pillars of Business

  • Value Creation: Offer something that people want or need.
  • Marketing: Reach the right audience for your product or service.
  • Sales: Prepare people to buy your product or service.
  • Value Delivery: Keep your customers satisfied.
  • Finances: Earn and manage money to run your business.

  • 12 Ways to Provide Value: A goldmine for entrepreneurs, detailing ways like products, services, and subscriptions.
  • Innovative Marketing Ideas: This book shows how something as simple as a viral billboard could make a product attention-seeking.

What's Special About This Book?

Traditional MBA programs are very cost-and-time-influencing programs. Although budget-friendly and fast, the practical insights over the theory make The Personal MBA a good resource for self-learners and entrepreneurs.

Final Thoughts

The Personal MBA is worth reading for anyone who feels an MBA is too pricey but wants to know the basics of business. It's a simple, practical, and action-oriented book that teaches you to think differently about business.
